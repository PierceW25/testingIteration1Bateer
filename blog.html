What Makes A Home Smart

Defining the Smart Home

What exactly is a smart home? While it may seem like an entirely new concept, the notion has been around for a while. In short, a smart home is one that uses technology to solve problems, whether obvious or not and is created based on your specific needs and lifestyle. In the past, it was both quite difficult and expensive to make changes that ensured your house was optimized for you. Today, when you move into a house or apartment, or even just redefine the space you’re currently in, it's actually pretty easy to shop for technology that'll make your life easier. Alexa-enabled speakers and lights you can turn on with an app are among the most common examples of smart devices and are definitely a great way to get started, when creating a smarter space. But there are also less obvious ways to technologically tailor your home. For example:

  • Nest has sensors that can detect when people are entering or leaving the room and adjust the temperature accordingly;
  • August has locks that automatically open once your phone connects with Bluetooth;
  • Canary surveillance cameras integrate seamlessly into safety systems like Amazon Echo Show or Google Home Hub—just say "Alexa/OK Google" and ask Canary what's happening at home;
  • Sony TVs use Android TV OS software so users can easily integrate streaming services like Netflix or Spotify.

Smart Home and the Internet of Things (IoT)

The term “smart home” is often thrown around when describing a residence that has appliances, lighting or other features that can be controlled remotely by a phone or tablet. What makes these products “smart” is the ability to access them through the Internet of Things (IoT). IoT refers to a network of physical devices that have sensors and software that connect to a network and can communicate with each other. There are many IoT devices available today, including smartwatches, fitness trackers and even refrigerators.

What can you do with a smart home?

Smart home devices can do so much more than help you control your home remotely. It’s also possible to automate your home (like turning on the lights when you arrive), make your home more energy-efficient, and use AI to analyze video footage from security cameras in order to detect suspicious activity.

How are smart homes safer?

We know that, in a smart home, lights turn on and off based on the family's needs and personal preferences, that thermostats automatically adjust to suit the family's schedule, or mood, but another aspect of them that doesn’t necessarily immediately come to mind is how much safer they are than regular homes.

In a smart home, a fire alarm will sound as soon as smoke is detected—whether or not someone is present to hear it go off. The same can be said for camera systems and possible intrusions. A homeowner can monitor their home from anywhere in the world using a smartphone app, so they know if everything's in order even when they're away for work or vacation. Overall, smart homes provide peace of mind to their residents, knowing that nothing will happen without them being aware of it.

What is it that makes a home smart, compared to those that are not?

A smart home is the one that learns about your preferences, habits and behaviors, understands what you are saying and does things for you automatically. It knows when you’ve come back from the office, it knows when you have gone to bed and it knows when the lights have been left on. A smart home is not like a factory working on full automation. It has a heart. A smart home is the one which remembers you, anticipates your needs and fulfills them before you ask.
